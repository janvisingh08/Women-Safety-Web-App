Rape Laws (Section 375 & 376 IPC)

Rape is defined under Section 375 IPC, and the punishment is provided under Section 376 IPC, with severe penalties including life imprisonment.

Marital rape is not criminalized in India except when the wife is below 18 years of age. The 2012 Nirbhaya case led to amendments increasing punishments for rape offenders.

Molestation (Section 354 IPC)

Molestation falls under Section 354 IPC, which protects women from assault or criminal force intended to outrage modesty.

The law was strengthened post the Vishakha case and includes punishments ranging from one to five years of imprisonment.

Domestic Violence (DV Act, 2005)

The Domestic Violence Act, 2005 protects women from physical, emotional, sexual, and financial abuse by family members.

This law allows victims to seek protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief from the abuser.

Human Trafficking (Section 370 IPC)

Section 370 IPC criminalizes trafficking of persons for exploitation, with severe punishments.

The Immoral Traffic Prevention Act (ITPA) provides additional protections against human trafficking and forced prostitution.

Cyber Crime Against Women (IT Act)

Under the IT Act, offenses like cyberstalking, online harassment, and revenge porn are punishable.

Section 66E, 67, and 67A of the IT Act deal with publishing and transmitting obscene content online, ensuring protection against digital harassment.

Prominent Cases & Verdicts

Nirbhaya Case (2012): Brutal gang-rape in Delhi led to the Criminal Law Amendment Act, 2013, introducing stricter rape laws.

Shakti Mills Case: Gang-rape of a journalist in Mumbai; resulted in life imprisonment for the accused.

Vishakha vs State of Rajasthan: Established workplace sexual harassment guidelines (Vishakha Guidelines).

Mathura Rape Case: A minor tribal girl was raped in police custody; led to reforms in custodial rape laws.

Tarun Tejpal Case: High-profile sexual assault case involving a journalist; highlighted the importance of workplace sexual harassment laws.

Priyadarshini Mattoo Case: Law student murdered by stalker; resulted in life imprisonment for the accused and highlighted the need for better stalking laws.
